Prior to coming to Waterloo, my musical experience was extremely casual. I was a very passionate shower singer, and I sang and played guitar in a small band with friends - we called ourselves TEAM - which was super fun but we didn't take ourselves too seriously. My friend and I heard about The WBs, and honestly I wasn't even going to audition, but he convinced me to do it for fun. Voila, I had an audition slot for the next week..

How did it go??

My friend and I had our auditions at around 8PM, so we got there early to warm up, and he was up first. Since we were doing it for jokes, I didn't think I'd be as nervous as I was. But these guys were legends. I mean they literally made it to Finals (top 10 Worldwide) last year, so I was definitely intimidated. I remember pacing around in circles until it was finally my turn.

We started with a range test, something I had never actually done before. I was able to hit a G4 and an Eb2. At the time I had zero idea what this meant, but hey turns out this is pretty decent! We then tried my falsetto, but I was literally shaking from the nerves, so instead of falsetto I made like a small frog sound ðŸ˜¬ Oops.

Next up was the solo pieces. I had prepared two songs: Locked out of Heaven by Bruno Mars and Perfect by Ed Sheeran. I ended up having to sing the first song multiple times, each one progressively higher than the next, and damn that stressed me out. Overall though, I thought they went pretty well!

Finally, I was handed a piece of sheet music. Then they told me to sing it. I was like ok this is where I die it's okay I've made it pretty far. They were very helpful though, and worked with me through the sight reading section of the audition. At the end, I got offered a Timbit and left on an emotional high.

Amazingly, that night we both got emails inviting us to callbacks! After that, I was accepted into the group as a Baritone, and suddenly I had joined the A Cappella world at Waterloo. Pretty neat right âœŒ

ICCA Quarterfinals @ UWaterloo

On February 18th, 2018, we competed in the International Championship of Collegiate A Cappella (ICCA). This term, the majority of our members were new as the older ones had left for their co-op terms. I was honoured to be chosen as Section Leader for the Baritone 1s, and led by our Musical Directors Yamen and Wes, we began our preparation. Because of the large A Cappella club here at UWaterloo(biggest in Canada, actually), the ICCA Quarterfinals were held here! Of the 10 teams to compete, the top two teams would then advance to Semifinals, and only the 1st place team would progress to Finals.

It was an amazing experience being able to see other A Cappella groups from around Ontario perform. After our performance, we were happy with ourselves, but we were so sure that the other groups were going to win. Contrary to this though, we placed second! This meant that we were advancing to Semifinals! We surpassed all expectations, and I remember the feeling of hearing our name announced on stage - what an incredible feeling. Next up: Queen's A Cappella Competition! We wanted to use this as an opportunity to fine-tune our performance, so we packed our bags and headed to Queen's University.

Queen's A Cappella Competition

The venue at Queen's was incredible. The stage had seats a full 360Â° around the stage, so the audience would be watching from all directions. My friend's from Kingston and he was pretty happy to hear that I enjoyed the city's landmark university.

After a very loud 3 hour carride full of singers singing badly but also really well at times, we got to our destination and met the other groups performing. They were mostly from Queen's, Waterloo, and a team from McGill was also there. My personal favourite performance was by the Trillers, who ended up placing 2nd. We placed third, behind In Full Colour, another team from UW that also won the ICCA Quarterfinals. Overall? Pretty fun weekend. I got to see my friends that go to Queen's, and spent some quality bonding time with the boys.

ICCA Semifinals @BostonU

Big finale time. Winning here meant we'd get the chance to perform on stage at Broadway Theatre, New York, in front of the world. We were all insanely excited to go - it's not everyday you get to travel to the States to compete in a singing competition.

The semifinals were hosted at Boston University, which by the way has an enormous campus. I learned this by spending a considerable portion of the day getting lost driving around, trying to find the allocated parking spots. I remember getting food at this one burrito store that charged way too much for a chicken wrap, and on top of that I lost the change. Tasted a solid 6.5/10 though.

Competition day came upon us, and we spent the morning warming up, doing soundcheck, and checking out the venue. Boston University's theatre was huge, and almost demanded importance. After some final preparations, the competition began! We were listed to go during the first half, and after listening to some performances by other groups, it was clear that the bar had been raised. A lot. It was finally our turn to perform. I still won't forget the feeling of going on stage and performing the set we had worked so hard to perfect. Every member left it all on stage, and I am so proud of what we were able to accomplish. You can view our performance here .

After the break, we had the opportunity to watch the remaining groups perform. I was absolutely floored by the talent showcased that day. Unfortunately, we didn't end up placing. The Potsdam Pitches won 1st place, followed by our very own In Full Colour from Waterloo! The competition was amazing, and this is definitely an experience I will never forget.
